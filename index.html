Next25 / Parliamentary Discourse Research
Research Reference
Overview
Research Aims
To understand more deeply the impact of our programs. Specifically, what are the most significant changes from undertaking a Next25 program for alumni? And do these changes produce observable changes to parliamentary discourse?
Context
Given that a large portion of our alumni are from the NSW parliament, the focus remains on the NSW parliament. However, once we have a baseline in NSW, there may be a possibility to investigate other parliaments.
Overall Methodology: Exploratory Sequential Mixed Methods Design (Creswell & Plano Clark, 2018) — an initial qualitative phase that informs and shapes a subsequent quantitative phase.
Phase 1 — Qualitative Exploration
Methodology
Semi-structured interviews with a sample of NSW program alumni.
Objective
These interviews will explore how participants perceive the program's influence on their parliamentary practice, the language and behaviours they associate with change, and the contexts in which those changes are most apparent.

Phase 2 — Quantitative Analysis
Methodology
Drawing on the qualitative findings, apply computational text analysis (AI/NLP) to Hansard records to examine discourse patterns across qualitative target domains.
Resources / Inputs
  • NSW Parliament Hansard API
  • Coding scheme / framework
  • Codebook
  • Coded transcript dataset with metadata and confounding variables

About Next25
Next25 is an Australian think-and-do tank focused on ensuring Australia has what it needs to make the future it wants. Founded in 2013, our Leadership programs have worked with parliamentarians across all Australian jurisdictions, aiming to empower politicians to lead with integrity and confidence in their values and to understand their power as individuals to improve the system.
